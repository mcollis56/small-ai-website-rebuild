Lesson 2: Your First Prompt

How to Talk to AI (It's Easier Than You Think)

A "prompt" is just the words you type into the AI. Think of it like giving instructions to a helpful employee.

Simple Prompt Formula

1
Tell the tool what you want

"Write an email..." or "Create a list of..." or "Help me explain..."

2
Add context (the details)

Who is it for? What's the situation? Any specific details?

3
Ask for a clear format

"Keep it short" or "Make it friendly" or "Use bullet points"

3 Ready-to-Use Prompts (Copy and Try These Now)

Prompt 1

Customer Follow-Up

Write a friendly follow-up email to a customer who came in last week. Thank them for their business, ask if everything was satisfactory, and invite them to come back. Keep it warm but professional. Make it 3-4 sentences.
Prompt 2

Social Media Post

Create an Instagram post for my small business announcing our weekend opening hours. We're open Saturday 9am-3pm and Sunday 10am-2pm. Make it friendly and use 2-3 relevant emojis. Keep it short.
Prompt 3

Professional Introduction

Write a brief introduction about my business for our website. We're a family-owned [YOUR BUSINESS TYPE] serving the Northern Beaches for [NUMBER] years. We focus on quality work and personal service. Make it warm and trustworthy, about 50 words.

üí° Pro Tip

If the AI's first answer isn't quite right, just type "make it shorter" or "make it more friendly" or "add more details." You can have a conversation with it!

Lesson 4: What AI Cannot Do

Important: You're Still the Boss

AI is a powerful tool, but it's not perfect. Here's what you need to know to use it safely and effectively.

üë§

1. You Stay in Control

AI suggests, you decide. Always read what the AI writes before sending it. It might sound good but miss your personal touch or get a detail wrong.

Example: AI might write a professional email, but you know your customer prefers casual language. You can adjust it.

‚úì

2. Always Check Everything

AI can make mistakes. It might get facts wrong, make up information, or misunderstand what you asked for.

Example: If AI writes a quote with prices, double-check the numbers. If it mentions a date, confirm it's correct.

üîí

3. Keep Private Data Safe

Don't put sensitive information into AI tools. No customer addresses, phone numbers, payment details, or private business information.

‚úÖ Safe: "Write an email to a customer about a delayed delivery"
‚ùå Not Safe: "Write an email to John Smith at 123 Beach Road about his $5,000 invoice"
ü§ù

4. It Can't Replace Human Touch

AI doesn't understand feelings or relationships like you do. Use it for the boring stuff, but add your personal touch for important communications.

Example: AI can draft a sympathy message, but you should add personal words that show you care.

‚è∞

5. It Doesn't Know Everything

AI's knowledge has limits. It doesn't know about events after its training date, your specific business details, or your local area specifics.

Example: It won't know about Northern Beaches council regulations or your business's actual opening hours unless you tell it.

‚öñÔ∏è

6. You're Responsible

If something goes wrong, it's your responsibility. Don't blame the AI‚Äîyou're the one who sent the message or made the decision.

Think of it like spell-check: it helps, but you're still responsible for what you send.

üåü The Golden Rule

Use AI to save time on tasks that don't need a personal touch. Always add your own judgment, care, and personality before hitting send.
